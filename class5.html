Media Writing

This course addresses the fundamental building blocks of writing for various forms of Media including news, features, press releases, and advertising copy for broadcast, print, and public relations media. In addition to writing with accuracy, students will learn how to gather and organize information while working under deadline pressure. Writings will reflect correct English language usage, including spelling, grammar, style consistency, and accuracy.

Professor: Evan Johnson

Evan Johnson is an instructor in the School of Communication, Multimedia Journalism courses specifically. In addition to teaching at Virginia Tech, Evan also has experience teaching at the University of Tennessee. He has more than 20 years of broadcast journalism experience as an executive producer, news anchor, and reporter. He has earned numerous awards, including a regional Edward R. Murrow Award from the Radio Television & Digital News Association, and awards from the Louisiana Association of Broadcasters, Virginia Association of Broadcasters, and the Louisiana-Mississippi AP Broadcasters Association. Mr. Johnson is a proud member of Kappa Alpha Psi Fraternity, Inc. and serves on the Board of Directors for the Kirk Family YMCA in Roanoke, VA. He is a New Orleans Saints fan, has a dog named Banjo, and loves cooking, his favorite meal being gumbo.
