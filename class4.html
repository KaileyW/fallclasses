Introduction to Communication

This course aims to survey of the communication discipline across areas of specialization from interpersonal to mediated and mass communication, including history and fundamental concepts, theories, and contexts. It places emphasis on ethical human behavior and message analysis.

Professor: Dr. Buddy Howell

Buddy Howell is an advanced instructor in the School of Communication. He is a national, university, and department award-winning teacher, receiving the 2024 Excellence in Teaching First-Year Seminars Award from the National Resource Center for the First-Year Experience and Students in Transition along with many others. Dr. Howell’s research interests include the rhetoric of U.S. Presidential public diplomacy and U.S. Presidential campaign and election rhetoric. His work has been published in Rhetoric & Public Affairs, the American Behavioral Scientist, the Southern Communication Journal, and Kaid and Holtz-Bacha, ed., Encyclopedia of Political Communication. He is a big football fan, enjoys reading, and watching movies. His favorite books are the Lord of the Rings and the Bible, and one of his favorite movies is the Princess Bride.
